Project and data

Project background

  • Next-generation sequencing was used to perform a detailed comparative analysis of the Ötzi genome in relation to genomic data of modern humans.
  • In addition to human DNA, a considerable amount of bacterial sequence data was generated here, including sequences that could be assigned to Borrelia burgdorferi.

Hypothesis

Based on the above background information, the following hypothesis could be formulated, which needs to be tested:

Ötzi fell ill with Lyme disease.

Workflow and technical report

Workflow

All the tools needed for the workflow of the project and their dependencies on each other are shown in the following plot:

Steps of the workflow and tools used in the workflow:

  • Quality control (FastQC & MultiQC)
  • Trimming (Trim Galore)
  • Mapping (bowtie2)
  • SAM to BAM and BAM-Index (samtools)
  • Reconstruction of mitochondrial DNA (samtools)
  • Annotation of reconstructed mtDNA (prokka)
  • Identification of the 5'/3' substitution rates (MapDamage)
  • Identification of haplogroups (haplogrep)
  • Multiple sequence alignment against references of AmtDB (Clustal Omega)
  • Phylogenetic tree (Biopython)
